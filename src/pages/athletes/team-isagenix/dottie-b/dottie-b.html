Fueled For Gold

You would never know from Dottie’s accomplishments that she’s a double lung and kidney transplant recipient. The determined athlete has won numerous gold medals in the 100/200-meter sprint at the USA Track and Field and Transplant Olympics. A mother of a young son, Dottie is also a fitness competitor who will be taking a crew of Isagenix® athletes to the TriFitness Challenge, a competition that combines a military-based obstacle course with a grace and physique element.

"Being born with cystic fibrosis and told I wasn’t going to be able to do this or that, I knew I had a different road for myself," shares the 45-year-old. "I always wanted to be an athlete, so nutrition has always been the difference between living just an OK life to an exceptional one."

Dottie and her husband, a specialty performance coach who trains pro athletes, have noticed big results in her training since switching to Isagenix in 2011. Along with sustained energy throughout the day, the track and field competitor experienced greater strength during sprints, quicker recovery and increased mental clarity—key for a sprinter who needs to react to the starting gun. "We did our own research and Founder and Master Formulator John Anderson and the Isagenix scientists just got it right." This summer Dottie will compete at the World Transplant Olympics in the 100/200-meter sprint, where she took gold in 2010. And she is confident she will win gold again. "Isagenix has allowed me to continue to live at an exceptional level of health."
