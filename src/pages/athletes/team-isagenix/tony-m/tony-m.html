Ex-NFL Player Shares His Passion for Health, Fitness and Photography

Tony shot to fame in 1989 as the first-round draft pick for the Green Bay Packers. He was touted as being one of Michigan State University’s best football players and his performance received numerous accolades. He weighed 308 pounds, could bench press 225 pounds 39 times and was nicknamed "The Incredible Bulk" by Sports Illustrated magazine.

Calling the Play

In 1999, Tony retired from professional football with a shoulder injury. He went on to marry his Michigan State University sweetheart, Char, and the two moved to Arizona in 2005. That’s where he met his now Isagenix® sponsor Jill B., and started on a 30-Day Cleansing and Fat Burning System.

"Initially I was skeptical about the products and opportunity, but now people always ask me what kind of supplements I take while I am at the gym," shares this father of four. "I tell them, Isagenix®."

His passion for health and fitness made sharing the products a no-brainer. Little did he know he was inadvertently creating a fast-growing Isagenix business by sharing his love for the products at the gym.

A Man in Motion

After receiving $500* in residual income, Tony was persuaded to put more time aside each week to build his Isagenix business. He started by opening his own professional photography studio to share Isagenix with potential Associates and host regular opportunity meetings.

"I tell my team that if they get involved and want success, I will demand a lot of them," explains Tony. "But, I will make sure they succeed."

Today, this 2-Star Golden Circle and 1-Star Crystal Executive puts in the same level of commitment and holds himself accountable as a leader by staying knowledgeable about the products and business. He does so by tuning into weekly podcasts, attending Isagenix events and staying in touch with his team frequently.

The money he earns from his Isagenix business allows him to continue pursuing his passion, photography.

"Education and effort are the keys to success and I really enjoy sharing my story and teaching people about how to get healthier," adds Tony. "The money is a bonus."
